The first version of Hovio was too clever.

We built sophisticated mood tracking dashboards, elaborate onboarding flows, detailed analytics about your emotional patterns presented in beautiful charts. We were proud of it. Our early testers told us it felt overwhelming, cold, and — ironically — stressful to use.

That feedback broke something open for us.

## The trap of building for builders

When you're a technical team building a product, you build for people like you. You appreciate complexity. You find data visualisations reassuring. You trust systems.

Most people seeking therapy are not in that headspace. They're often in pain. They want simplicity, warmth, and the feeling that something — or someone — is listening.

We had built a product that demonstrated our capabilities rather than serving their needs. It took us several uncomfortable conversations to see it clearly.

## What we stripped out

The dashboard went first. Nobody needed to see a graph of their mood over time when they were having a hard day. That's not support — that's surveillance dressed as care.

The lengthy onboarding followed. We'd been asking people to fill in detailed psychological profiles before they'd experienced any value from the platform. The commitment we were asking for wasn't earned yet.

We stripped it back until what remained was a single question: *How are you feeling right now?*

## What stayed

The AI memory stayed. The core insight — that continuity of context is one of the most valuable things technology can offer in mental health care — held up under scrutiny. People don't want to repeat themselves. They want to be remembered.

The matching system stayed. Our belief that the right therapist match is transformative, and that AI can dramatically improve the quality of that match, only grew stronger as we spoke to more people.

The 24/7 availability stayed. The 2am moments are real. They matter enormously.

## What we learned

Build for the person, not the product. When you're designing for someone who's vulnerable, the standard for clarity, warmth, and simplicity is higher than anywhere else in tech.

If your product adds friction to someone seeking support, you've already failed — no matter how impressive the technology underneath.

We're still learning. But we're learning faster now.
