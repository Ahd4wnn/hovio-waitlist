Mental health awareness has never been higher. Mental health access has barely moved.

This is the central tension defining the space in 2025 — and it's the problem Hovio exists to solve.

## The numbers

The World Health Organization estimates that approximately 970 million people globally live with a mental health disorder. Of those, the treatment gap in low- and middle-income countries sits above 75%. In high-income countries — places with established healthcare systems — it's still above 50%.

Average wait times for a first therapy appointment in the United Kingdom now exceed three months through public services. In the United States, the median cost of a private therapy session has risen to over $200, with insurance coverage remaining inconsistent and confusing.

These aren't new problems. But they're not improving at anywhere near the rate that awareness is growing.

## Why awareness without access is dangerous

There's a cruelty to telling someone that help exists, encouraging them to reach out, and then offering them a four-month waitlist.

The peak moment of someone's willingness to seek help — the moment they've overcome stigma, fear, and inertia — is exactly when access barriers do the most damage. Miss that window, and many people don't try again for years.

## What technology can and cannot fix

Technology cannot train more therapists. That takes time, infrastructure, and policy — things that move slowly.

What technology can do is dramatically expand the surface area of support available to people right now, while the system catches up. It can provide the 2am conversation. It can provide the continuity of memory between sessions. It can remove the matching guesswork that sends people to therapists who aren't right for them.

None of this is a substitute for a well-funded, accessible mental health system. But it's what's possible today, built with care.

That's what we're trying to do.
